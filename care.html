Dog Care Guide

Learn how to take proper care of your dog for a healthy and happy life.

Feeding Guide

The Balanced Bowl

Dogs are omnivores. They need balance of proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals containing food like us. Look for "Complete and Balanced".

Toxic Food

Never feed your dog chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, xylitol (sweetener), or cooked bones which can splinter.

Hydration

Always provide fresh, clean water. A general rule is 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily.

Grooming Routine

i. Brushing

Brush weekly to reduce shedding and prevent matting. It also helps distribute natural oils for a shiny coat.

ii. Bathing

Bathe only when necessary (every 1-3 months) using dog-specific shampoo to maintain pH balance.

iii. Nail Trimming

Trim nails monthly. If you hear them clicking on the floor, they are too long!

Health and Wellness

Vaccinations

Core vaccines (Rabies, DHPP) are essential. Ask your vet about non-core lifestyle vaccines.

Parasite Control

Year-round prevention for fleas, ticks, and heartworm is cheaper than the cure.

Checkups

Annual exams for adults, bi-annual for seniors. Early detection saves lives.

Dental Care

Dental disease is common. Brush teeth regularly and provide dental chews.
